Laying the Foundation

You can’t build a great building on a weak foundation. You must have a solid foundation if you’re going to have a strong superstructure 
Gordon B. Hinckley
October 4, 2018
I’ve always been fascinated by builders. I remember when I was 12 years old and my granddad and I worked on building a shed in the backyard of the house in which I was raised. I was still fairly young so back then I didn’t understand the importance of what we were doing. I didn’t fully grasp the concept of building something from the ground up with your own hands and using it for its designed purpose. Now that I’m older I understand the importance of building something, and taking care of your creation. With this new mindset and my knowledge and understanding of technology and applications, It’s time to begin building something new. Something that will go by the name of OneFit.

OneFit will be a tool focused on helping trainers engage with their clients in a more efficient and client focus fashion. With this app a trainer will be able to create a profile for their clients storing the client’s goals, stats, preferences, workout history, and timelines. The trainer will have the ability to curate a workout for the client from a list compiled with client’s and the trainer’s favorite workouts, The trainer will also be able to prescribe the amount of sets and reps for each workout. Once the trainer has posted the workout, the client will receive a notification and be able to review the workout and ask the message the trainer with any questions or comments they may have. When the client is ready to do the workout, they will be able to track the workout with examples and step by step instructions on how to do the prescribed workout. Each client will have access to their personal profile and be able to see and edit stats, view progress graphs and workout history.

Now to the nuts and bolts of this thing. In all honesty, I am not a fan of front end development. When it comes to front development I tend to stay away from it unless it’s drag and drop [like my website :) ]. For this project, since I want it to be done correctly and as perfect as possible, I will stay away for the front end dev portion of it and focus more on backend development and infrastructure. I’ve started writing the the backend for this project in GoLang and all of the APIs will be setup through this domain for right now. When I have a solid set of APIs setup and tested, I will purchase the OneFit domain and begin running the APIs through there. I will also use the time to setup the continuous integration and development infrastructure for the app.

I know this is a lot of work and possibly very unnecessary with all of the tools out there to help build apps like this. I also fully expect this idea to evolve as it is being developed, but I want to use this as a learning opportunity. I also want to lay the foundation for the app so whenever someone comes in to work on the app, there will be a simple and basic infrastructure setup for easy on-boarding.

I will write about the development process as it happens. There will also be a time on my website explaining what tasks I’ve completed and when the task was completed. Please feel free to reach out with any questions, comments, and advice.
